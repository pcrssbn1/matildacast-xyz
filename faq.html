Frequently Asked Questions About Matilda Cast

The various productions of Matilda have featured hundreds of talented performers over the years, from the iconic 1996 film to the award-winning stage musical and the recent Netflix adaptation. Understanding the casting decisions, performer backgrounds, and production details helps fans appreciate the artistry involved in bringing Roald Dahl's beloved character to life.

Below you'll find detailed answers to the most common questions about Matilda cast members across different productions. These responses draw from official production records, interviews with cast members, and documented theatrical history.

What is Matilda cast?

Matilda cast refers to the actors and performers who star in productions of the musical 'Matilda,' based on Roald Dahl's beloved children's book, as well as film adaptations. The term encompasses everyone from the young actresses who play the title character to supporting roles like Miss Trunchbull, Miss Honey, Mr. and Mrs. Wormwood, and the ensemble of schoolchildren. Since the story has been adapted multiple times—including the 1996 film starring Mara Wilson, the stage musical that premiered in 2010, and the 2022 Netflix musical film—there are numerous casts associated with the Matilda name. Professional theater productions typically employ multiple young actresses to rotate in the lead role, making the cast dynamic and ever-changing. The casting also varies significantly between productions in different cities and countries, with West End, Broadway, touring, and regional theater productions each having their own complete cast of performers.

Who plays Matilda in the musical?

The role of Matilda in stage musical productions is typically performed by multiple young actresses who rotate in the part, rather than a single performer. This rotation system exists because of child labor laws, the demanding nature of the role, and the need to protect young performers from burnout. The original West End production featured four Matildas: Cleo Demetriou, Eleanor Worthington Cox, Kerry Ingram, and Sophia Kiely. The Broadway premiere similarly cast four girls: Sophia Gennusa, Oona Laurence, Bailey Ryon, and Milly Shapiro. Current productions continue this tradition, with each theater company casting three to four actresses for the role. The 2022 Netflix film featured Irish actress Alisha Weir as the sole Matilda, since film productions don't require the same rotation system. Touring productions and international stagings have their own casts, meaning dozens of young performers have had the opportunity to play Matilda professionally since the musical's 2010 premiere.

Where can I find information about Matilda cast members?

Information about Matilda cast members can be found through several authoritative sources. Official theater websites for venues currently running the show provide the most up-to-date cast lists, including which actresses are performing on specific dates. The Internet Broadway Database (IBDB.com) maintains comprehensive records of all Broadway cast members throughout the show's run from 2013 to 2017. For the West End production, the Official London Theatre website and individual venue sites list current performers. Playbills distributed at performances contain complete cast and crew information. The Internet Movie Database (IMDb.com) provides detailed filmographies for the 1996 film cast and the 2022 Netflix adaptation. Theater news websites like Playbill.com and BroadwayWorld.com regularly publish casting announcements and interviews with performers. Social media accounts managed by official productions often highlight cast members and share behind-the-scenes content. For historical research, theater archives and the Roald Dahl Story Company website offer context about various productions and their performers.

How old are the actors who play Matilda?

Child actors who play Matilda in professional productions are typically between 8 and 12 years old, though this can vary slightly depending on the specific production and local regulations. The character herself is described as being five years old in the story, but casting directors seek slightly older performers who possess the vocal maturity, stamina, and acting skills required for the demanding role. In the 1996 film, Mara Wilson was 8 years old when filming began. The original West End Matildas ranged from 9 to 11 years old at the time of their casting. Alisha Weir was 13 during the filming of the 2022 Netflix adaptation, slightly older than typical stage casting but appropriate for the film's production schedule. Professional productions must comply with child labor laws that restrict working hours and require on-set education, which influences casting decisions. The rotation system used in stage productions specifically accommodates the needs of young performers, ensuring they can maintain their education and have adequate rest between performances while still delivering consistent, high-quality work.

Are there different Matilda casts for different productions?

Yes, each theater production of Matilda has its own distinct cast, with Broadway, West End, touring productions, regional theaters, and international stagings all featuring different performers. Even within a single production, the cast changes over time as contracts end and new performers are hired. The original West End production that opened in 2011 had a completely different cast from the Broadway production that premiered in 2013, though director Matthew Warchus and some creative team members remained consistent. North American tours, Australian productions, and versions staged in countries like South Korea, the Philippines, and across Europe each conduct their own casting processes and feature local talent. Additionally, within any given production, multiple actresses rotate in the title role, meaning audiences might see different Matildas depending on which performance they attend. This diversity of casting is one of the musical's strengths, as it allows for varied interpretations of the character while maintaining the core story and musical elements. Film adaptations have their own separate casts entirely—the 1996 movie and 2022 Netflix film share no performers despite adapting the same source material.

Who played Miss Trunchbull in different Matilda productions?

Miss Trunchbull has been portrayed by several notable actors across different Matilda productions, each bringing unique qualities to the tyrannical headmistress. In the 1996 film, British actress Pam Ferris created an iconic interpretation that emphasized physical intimidation and dark comedy. For the stage musical, Bertie Carvel originated the role in both the West End (2011) and Broadway (2013) productions, winning both an Olivier Award and a Tony Award for his performance. Carvel's portrayal followed the theatrical tradition of having male actors play the character, adding to the comedic and grotesque elements. The 2022 Netflix film cast Emma Thompson in the role, with the acclaimed actress undergoing significant physical transformation through prosthetics and costumes. Various touring and international productions have featured different actors, including Dan Chameroy in the North American tour and Craige Els in later West End performances. The role requires exceptional physical comedy skills, vocal power, and the ability to be genuinely threatening while remaining within the bounds of family entertainment, making it one of the most challenging and sought-after character roles in contemporary musical theater.

What happened to the 1996 Matilda cast members?

The cast members from the 1996 Matilda film have taken diverse paths since the movie's release. Mara Wilson, who played the title character, continued acting through childhood with roles in films like 'Miracle on 34th Street' and 'Thomas and the Magic Railroad' before stepping away from on-screen acting in her teens. She later became a writer, publishing a memoir titled 'Where Am I Now?' in 2016 and contributing to various publications. Danny DeVito has remained a prolific actor, director, and producer, appearing in shows like 'It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia' and numerous films. Rhea Perlman continues to work steadily in television and film. Pam Ferris has had a successful career in British television, appearing in series like 'Call the Midwife' and 'Rosemary & Thyme.' Embeth Davidtz has maintained an active film career with roles in 'Fracture,' 'The Amazing Spider-Man,' and other productions. Many of the child actors who played Matilda's classmates left the entertainment industry to pursue other careers. Jimmy Karz, who played Bruce Bogtrotter in the memorable chocolate cake scene, attended college and works outside of acting. The cast occasionally reunites for retrospective interviews and anniversary celebrations, reflecting on the film's enduring legacy and impact on their lives.

How are Matildas cast for the musical?

Casting young actresses for the role of Matilda in stage productions involves an extensive and rigorous audition process. Production companies typically hold open calls in the cities where shows will run, seeking girls between ages 8 and 12 who can sing, act, and move well. Initial auditions assess vocal range and quality, as the role requires strong singing ability across challenging musical numbers like 'Naughty' and 'Quiet.' Callbacks involve acting scenes, learning choreography, and sometimes improvisation to evaluate a performer's ability to embody Matilda's intelligence, determination, and vulnerability. Casting directors specifically look for girls who can convey the character's extraordinary intellect and emotional depth while remaining believable as a child. According to interviews with casting directors, they receive thousands of submissions for each production and typically audition hundreds of candidates before selecting the final group. Most productions cast three to four Matildas who will rotate in the role, so directors assess not only individual talent but also how the group of performers can maintain consistency in the character portrayal. Selected actresses then undergo several weeks of intensive rehearsal, working with vocal coaches, choreographers, and directors to prepare for the demanding role before beginning performances.
